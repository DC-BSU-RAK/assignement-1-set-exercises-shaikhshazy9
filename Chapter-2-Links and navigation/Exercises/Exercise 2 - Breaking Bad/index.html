Breaking Bad

The story of a chemistry teacher turned meth kingpin

Official Website

Overview

Breaking Bad is an American neo-Western crime drama television series created and produced by Vince Gilligan. Set and filmed in Albuquerque, New Mexico, the series follows Walter White, a struggling and depressed high school chemistry teacher who is diagnosed with stage-3 lung cancer.

To secure his family's financial future before he dies, Walter turns to a life of crime and partners with a former student, Jesse Pinkman, to produce and distribute crystal meth. The show explores how a fatal diagnosis releases a typical man from the daily concerns and constraints of normal society and follows his transformation from mild-mannered family man to a kingpin of the drug trade.

Main Characters

Walter White

A high school chemistry teacher diagnosed with inoperable lung cancer who turns to manufacturing methamphetamine to secure his family's financial future. As his cancer goes into remission and he becomes more successful in the drug trade, Walter deviates from his original kind-hearted self and becomes increasingly ruthless.

Jesse Pinkman

A former student of Walter White who becomes his business partner in the meth trade. Though he initially seems immoral and indifferent, Jesse is actually emotionally fragile and later develops a conscience that leads him to struggle with their criminal lifestyle.

Skyler White

Walter's wife who initially works as a bookkeeper and later learns of Walter's criminal activities. She goes from being completely against Walter's criminal life to becoming complicit in his money laundering operations.

Hank Schrader

Walter's brother-in-law and a DEA agent who leads the investigation of the meth dealer known as "Heisenberg," unaware that he is actually pursuing Walter.
