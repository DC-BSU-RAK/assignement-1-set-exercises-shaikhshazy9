Classic Homemade Lasagna

This hearty homemade lasagna is the perfect comfort food for family gatherings or special occasions. Layers of rich meat sauce, creamy cheese mixture, and perfectly cooked pasta create an Italian masterpiece that's worth every minute spent in the kitchen.

Ingredients

  • 12 lasagna noodles
  • 1 pound ground beef (preferably 80/20 lean-to-fat ratio)
  • 1 pound Italian sausage, casings removed
  • 1 large yellow onion, finely diced
  • 5 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 cans (28 ounces each) crushed San Marzano tomatoes
  • 2 cans (6 ounces each) tomato paste
  • 2/3 cup water
  • 1/4 cup red wine (optional)
  • 3 tablespoons fresh basil, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • 2 teaspoons dried oregano
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 2 teaspoons brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
  • 4 cups whole milk ricotta cheese
  • 4 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 large eggs, lightly beaten
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

Method

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.
  2. Cook lasagna noodles according to package directions until al dente (firm to bite). Drain and lay noodles flat on oiled baking sheets to prevent sticking.
  3. In a large heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add onions and cook until translucent, about 5 minutes.
  4. Add garlic and cook for 30 seconds until fragrant. Add ground beef and Italian sausage, breaking it apart with a wooden spoon. Cook until no longer pink, about 8-10 minutes.
  5. Drain excess fat if necessary. Add tomato paste and cook for 2 minutes, stirring constantly.
  6. Pour in crushed tomatoes, water, and wine (if using). Add basil, parsley, oregano, bay leaves, brown sugar, Italian seasoning, salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes.
  7. Bring to a simmer, then reduce heat to low. Cover partially and cook for 30-45 minutes, stirring occasionally, until sauce thickens.
  8. While sauce simmers, prepare the cheese mixture. In a large bowl, combine ricotta cheese, 2 cups of mozzarella, 1/2 cup of Parmesan cheese, eggs, and a pinch of salt and pepper. Mix well.
  9. Remove bay leaves from the sauce. Taste and adjust seasonings if needed.
  10. To assemble: Spread 1 cup of meat sauce on the bottom of a deep 9×13-inch baking dish.
  11. Arrange 3 noodles lengthwise over the sauce. Spread 1/3 of the ricotta mixture over the noodles, then 1/4 of the remaining meat sauce. Sprinkle with some mozzarella.
  12. Repeat layers twice more: noodles, ricotta mixture, meat sauce, and some mozzarella.
  13. Top with final layer of noodles, remaining meat sauce, remaining mozzarella, and all remaining Parmesan cheese.
  14. Cover with aluminum foil (spray the underside with cooking spray to prevent sticking to cheese).
  15. Bake covered for 25 minutes. Remove foil and bake for an additional 25 minutes until cheese is bubbly and golden brown.
  16. Remove from oven and let stand for 15 minutes before serving to allow lasagna to set properly.

Chef's Tips: For best results, make the sauce a day ahead to allow flavors to develop. You can also assemble the entire lasagna up to 24 hours in advance and refrigerate before baking. If baking from refrigerated, add about 10-15 minutes to the covered baking time.
